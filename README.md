# Labour Market Competitiveness and Skill Demand Analysis - Skills-Based-Alignment-of-Competitiveness-Indicators-with-Job-Demand


This repository contains the data, code, visualizations, and final report for my Master's thesis on labour market competitiveness and skill demand across sectors and countries.

The study combines employer-side concentration measures (HHI and CR4) with skill-based indicators (Location Quotients, Shannon Entropy, and Gini Coefficient) to examine how labour market structure relates to the skills requested in online job advertisements.

The analysis covers six sectors across seven countries and explores:

Labour market concentration and competitiveness
Sector-specific skill demand patterns
Core skill profiles based on Top-N skills
Cross-country comparisons
Statistical testing of sector differences
Relationships between competitiveness and skill composition
Robustness and sensitivity analyses

The repository includes:

Final thesis report
Processed datasets
Analysis scripts
Figures and visualizations
Supplementary materials

This work aims to provide a data-driven view of how employer concentration and skill demand interact across different sectors and labour markets.
